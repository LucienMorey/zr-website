Communication

Communication outside school hours/weekly meetings is important. It's especially important during the allience stage of the International Competition when they join with a team in another country (and time zone).

Here are a couple of options that see regular use from ZR teams:

Slack

Slack is a live chat platform designed for software and engineering teams. If you were doing a project such as this as a university team or a small company, this is the go-to piece of software.

Slack organises conversations in a series of 'channels' - you might create a #code channel for discussing progress and issues to do with the programming aspect of ZR, or a #strategy channel for discussing the strategy you will implement.

Slack also allows for a variety of addons and plugins - for example, a GitHub plugin that notifies a channel about new commits or issues with your code repository. One of the lead mentors has also written a Slack plugin that allows you to search within the Zero Robotics manual from inside Slack (listed above).

Discord

Discord is a similar live chat platform, but instead designed more for gamers. Discord doesn't have the variety of plugins and development tools as readily available as Slack (although many common Slack plugins can still be replicated inside Discord), but it makes up for that by having user-friendly, high-fidelity voice chat options. Being able to talk to each other over voice chat while coding, or discussing strategy, or coordinating meetings (especially with international teams) is a very useful option to have available.

If you have any other alternatives to these that you thought worked well, let us know! Send an email to zero-robotics.admin@sydney.edu.au.
