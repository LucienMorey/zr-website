A competition like no other

Zero Robotics is a multinational space-themed programming and robotics competition. Students design code to control a zero-gravity robot - known as SPHERES - play in a virtual game against other teams of students from around the world.

Early rounds of the competition are played on MIT's state-of-the-art zero-gravity simulator, but the final round of competition is played in space aboard the International Space Station itself. Since they are dealing with real systems, students have to handle uncertainties in operation, code size limitations, and other real world engineering problems.

Thanks to a collaboration between MIT, NASA and The University of Sydney we are proud to provide Zero Robotics to all Australian schools free of charge!

Schedule

The Australian Competition

The University of Sydney runs a pre-international competition around June-July based on a past game mode. This is open to all schools within Australia and New Zealand, and faces teams against each other on a leaderboard, in both 2D and 3D versions of the game environment.

The International Competition

Well-performing Australian teams will be paired with two other international teams to form an Alliance. MIT will release a new, unique game mode that hasn't been seen before, and Alliances will need to work together to design, develop, and implement their strategy, earning them their place in the Finals.

The Finals Event

In January, after the 3D Alliance round has run, the top 18 Alliances (comprising of a total of 54 teams) will face off against each other in the Finals. However, unlike the rest of the competition, the robots and their code are no longer virtual - their code will be run by real astronauts on real SPHERES stationed on the International Space Station.

The Game

Every year, MIT develops a game for the SPHERES to play. These games are themed around real or planned space endevours, such as collecting bacteria samples, or photographing asteroids.

Past examples of games include:

  • SpySPHERES: Take photos of points of interest on a large asteroid, while also photographing and avoiding being photographed by your opponent.
  • SPACE-S: Assemble pieces of a satellite, earning points and defending your zone against your opponent.
  • LifeSPHERES: Drill the surface of Enceladus to search for bacteria, attempting to gain more and better quality samples than your opponent. Watch out for geysers!
